Story

Uncharted 2: Among Thieves follows treasure hunter Nathan Drake as he searches for the lost fleet of Marco Polo and the mythical city of Shambhala. The story begins with Drake escaping a wrecked train hanging off a snowy cliff, then flashes back to explain how he got there. Months earlier, he joins his old associate Harry Flynn and former lover Chloe Frazer to steal an artifact linked to Marco Polo. After Flynn betrays him and frames him for the theft, Drake escapes prison with help from Victor “Sully” Sullivan and journalist Elena Fisher. Drake learns that ruthless war criminal Zoran Lazarević is also searching for Shambhala to gain its legendary power. Traveling across Istanbul, Borneo, Nepal, and the Himalayas, Drake uncovers clues revealing that the Cintamani Stone is actually a powerful resin from the Tree of Life. In the hidden city, Lazarević consumes the tree’s sap to gain superhuman strength, forcing Drake to confront him. Drake defeats Lazarević and chooses to walk away from the treasure, ultimately reconciling with Elena as Shambhala is destroyed.

Gameplay

  • Third-person action-adventure perspective
  • Platforming and climbing mechanics (ledge grabbing, wall climbing, rope swinging)
  • Puzzle-solving (ancient mechanisms, environmental clues, journal hints)
  • Cinematic set pieces (train sequences, collapsing buildings, chases)
  • Combat system (cover-based shooting, melee, stealth takedowns)
  • Variety of weapons (pistols, rifles, shotguns, grenades)
  • Environmental interaction (destructible cover, dynamic enemy placement)
  • Online multiplayer mode (competitive and cooperative gameplay)

Reception

Uncharted 2: Among Thieves* received widespread critical acclaim when it launched in 2009. Reviewers praised its cinematic presentation, storytelling, and strong character development. The game’s voice acting and motion capture performances were especially highlighted as industry-leading at the time. Critics also noted major improvements in combat, climbing mechanics, and overall pacing compared to the first game. Its set pieces and action sequences were frequently compared to big-budget Hollywood films. The newly introduced multiplayer mode was well received and added significant replay value. The game holds a 96/100 on Metacritic, indicating universal acclaim. It won numerous Game of the Year awards and is often regarded as one of the greatest video games ever made.
