Story

Nathan Drake is a modern-day treasure hunter who believes he is following in the footsteps of Sir Francis Drake. He searches for the legendary treasure of El Dorado after discovering clues that suggest it was hidden long ago. Nathan teams up with journalist Elena Fisher and his mentor Victor “Sully” Sullivan. Their journey leads them to a mysterious, abandoned island filled with ruins and danger. A ruthless treasure hunter named Gabriel Roman and his mercenaries pursue the same prize. As the search continues, the group uncovers signs that previous explorers met a terrible fate. El Dorado is revealed to be far more dangerous than a simple treasure. Nathan must stop the villains and escape the island alive.

Gameplay

  • Third-person action-adventure gameplay
  • Exploration of ruins, jungles, and abandoned structures
  • Platforming with climbing, jumping, and ledge traversal
  • Cover-based shooting and gunfights
  • Light puzzle-solving using the environment
  • Cinematic set pieces and scripted action moments
  • Linear level design with guided progression
  • Mix of combat, exploration, and storytelling

Reception

Uncharted: Drake’s Fortune received generally positive reviews from critics upon its release in 2007. It holds a Metacritic score of around 88/100, indicating strong overall approval. Reviewers praised its cinematic presentation and comparisons to adventure films like Indiana Jones. The voice acting, especially Nolan North’s performance as Nathan Drake, was widely acclaimed. Critics also highlighted the game’s impressive graphics and animation for an early PlayStation 3 title. The platforming and action sequences were considered engaging and well-paced. However, some reviewers criticized the repetitive enemy encounters in the later chapters. Despite its flaws, the game was seen as a strong new franchise debut and a major exclusive for the PS3.
